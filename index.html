About Bhutan

A small, landlocked country nestled deep in the Himalayas between India and China, Bhutan is characterized by steep mountains and deep valleys, which led to scattered population settlement patterns. The country is known for its unique philosophy – Gross National Happiness (GNH) – which guides its development strategy.

Choose Your Place

Thimphu

Thimphu is the capital city of Bhutan and the cultural and economic center of the country. It blends traditional Bhutanese architecture with modern development, offering visitors a unique cultural experience.


Some famous locations in Thimphu include:


  • Tashichho Dzong
  • Buddha Dordenma
  • National Memorial Chorten
  • Folk Heritage Museum
  • Motithang Takin Preserve
  • Changangkha Lhakhang
  • Simtokha Dzong
Paro

Paro is a picturesque town in Bhutan, located in the Paro Valley. It is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.


Some famous locations in Paro include:


  • Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest)
  • Rinpung Dzong
  • National Museum of Bhutan
  • Drukgyel Dzong
  • Kyichu Lhakhang
  • Dzongdrakha Temple
  • Jangsarbu Lhakhang
Punakha

Punakha is a scenic town in Bhutan, situated in the Punakha Valley. It is known for its beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and religious significance.


Some famous locations in Punakha include:


  • Punakha Dzong
  • Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten
  • Punakha Suspension Bridge
  • Sangchhen Dorji Lhuendrup Lhakhang Nunnery
  • Chimi Lhakhang
  • Talo Village
Wangdue

Wangdue is a picturesque town in Bhutan, located in the Wangdue Phodrang District. It is known for its scenic beauty, historical landmarks, and vibrant culture.


Some famous locations in Wangdue include:


  • Wangdue Phodrang Dzong
  • Gangtey Monastery
  • Phobjikha Valley
  • Wangdue Tshechu Festival
  • Gasleo Hot Springs
  • Khamsa Yulley Namgyal
Bumthang

Bumthang is a picturesque region in central Bhutan, known for its natural beauty, rich history, and spiritual significance. It is often referred to as the "spiritual heartland" of Bhutan.


Some famous locations in Bumthang include:


  • Jakar Dzong
  • Kurjey Lhakhang
  • Jambay Lhakhang
  • Tamzhing Monastery
  • Mebar Tsho (Burning Lake)
  • Red Panda Brewery
  • Swiss Farm
Chhukha

Chhukha is a district in southwestern Bhutan, known for its stunning landscapes, hydroelectric power projects, and cultural heritage.


Some famous locations in Chhukha include:


  • Chhukha Dzong
  • Tala Hydropower Plant
  • Amochhu Land Development Project
  • Chhukha Hydropower Plant
  • Chhukha Tshechu Festival
  • Phuentsholing City
  • Kharbandi Gompa
